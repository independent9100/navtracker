# 2026-07-08 — Imazu 22 encounter battery (identity through close crossings)

The 22 canonical **Imazu problem** encounter situations (Imazu, H., 1987, PhD
thesis, Univ. of Tokyo) transcribed as fixed-geometry sim scenarios
`imazu_01`..`imazu_22`: own-ship plus 1-3 target ships in every combination of
head-on / crossing / overtaking. This is a citable regression suite for exactly
what crossing geometry stresses in a tracker — **identity stability through
close passes**. Cases 1-4 are single-target (the four pure COLREG encounters),
5-11 two-target (7 cases), 12-22 three-target (11 cases): 4 + 7 + 11 = 22.

**Tracker-suite framing, not COLAV.** Own-ship steams a fixed course and never
manoeuvres; every target holds a constant course/speed. The encounters are
allowed to pass close or straight through collision geometry — that closeness at
CPA is the identity stress the suite exists to measure. Radar + AIS arm on every
vessel (no camera). Truth is the generator's independent ground truth (consumed
by no arm), so the accuracy numbers are honest by construction — the same
property `sim_multisensor` gives, applied to the crossing-geometry problem.

## Geometry source and conventions

Primary source: the **CORALL** repository `src/utils/imazu_cases.py`
(Klins101/CORALL, MIT), the runnable Sarhadi/Naeem-group encoding, verified by
direct download 2026-07-08. Its per-target positions/headings are transcribed
verbatim into `tests/fixtures/sim_multisensor/generator/imazu.py` (arithmetic
kept as-authored so it diffs against the source); the non-canonical `Case 23` is
dropped. CORALL frame: own-ship at (0,0) heading 0°=East; we keep that frame
(own-ship marine course 090) so CORALL `(x,y)` maps 1:1 onto ENU `(East,North)`.

**Speeds** — CORALL's hardcoded scalars (own 43.3, target 18.52) are
non-physical as m/s or kn, but their **ratio** is load-bearing: CORALL's
crossing geometry is tuned so a target collides with own-ship iff
`v_target/v_own = 18.52/43.3 = 0.4277` (check: Case 2 target at (5,-2.14) N-bound
reaches own's track when `2.14/v_t = 5/v_own`). We preserve that ratio and pick a
physical own-ship speed (**20 kn**, targets **8.6 kn**); absolute speed is a free
frame choice that leaves the encounters unchanged. Ranges are scaled ×0.5
(CORALL 3-7 NM → a realistic close-quarters 1.5-3.5 NM) so each case reaches CPA
in ~6-9 min; scaling is CPA-preserving up to the same factor and leaves all
bearings/courses/crossing angles identical.

**Sources disagree on scaling, not topology.** At least three independent numeric
encodings exist (CORALL; Waltz & Okhrin arXiv:2211.01004 Table D.1, own-North,
6.009 NM circle, has a Case-8==Case-5 typo; Xie JMSE 12(3):372, 21 rows only;
Lyu JMSE 12(8):1289, the only per-target knots table). They agree on the
topology (which encounters, the 4/7/11 grouping) and differ only on absolute
speed/frame/range — parameter-level, not geometry. Per the ticket we picked one
(CORALL, because it is verbatim-verifiable runnable code) and record the
divergence. Per-case COLREG-role tags below are **derived from geometry** (no
source publishes an explicit role table).

## Reproduce

Fixtures: seeded Python generation under `tests/fixtures/sim_multisensor/`
(local-only/git-ignored; `python -m generator.generate --family imazu`, see that
dir's README). Battery is 22 scenarios, seed 0. Metrics: GOSPA `c=20, p=α=2` (m);
OSPA cutoff 500 m; assoc gate 100 m. Aggregate `id_sw`/`breaks` are **mean per
truth track** (e.g. imazu_09 per-truth 25 & 32 → 28.5).

```
SIMMS_DIR=$PWD/tests/fixtures/sim_multisensor \
  ./build/bench/navtracker_bench_baseline --with-imazu --skip-replays \
  --scenario-filter imazu --config-eq <config> --seeds 1 --run-id <id> --out docs/baselines/
```

## Reading the numbers (findings — no tuning)

Per the ticket, **no config was tuned to make cases pretty**; a case where
identity churns is a finding, reported with the geometry named.

- **Single-target cases are clean for both trackers.** imazu_01-04 (head-on,
  crossing-give-way, overtaking, crossing-stand-on — the four pure COLREG
  encounters, and note the tags come out canonically correct, an
  end-to-end sanity check of the geometry conversion): 0 id-switches, GOSPA
  15-20, lifetime ≥0.97. One target → no association ambiguity → no churn.

- **MHT identity churns hard on dense multi-target crossings — direct evidence
  for backlog #11 (bearing-driven identity churn).** id-switches climb with
  target count and crossing density, from 0 (single-target) to double digits
  (2-target) to the worst cases:
  - **imazu_17** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **72.0** mean switches/truth
  - **imazu_20** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **68.0**
  - **imazu_21** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **43.0**
  - **imazu_19** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **42.3**
  - **imazu_14** (crossing-give-way ×2 + overtaking): **32.0**

  The pattern is unambiguous: the churn concentrates in the 3-target cases that
  put an **overtaken co-course target and one or more crossers converging on the
  same CPA region** (17/19/20/21 are the same overtaking-heavy family). When two
  or three targets pass within metres of each other and of own-ship — and radar
  cross-range error (~20-30 m at 1-2 NM) exceeds the true separation — MHT's
  per-hypothesis identity assignment thrashes. Position accuracy holds
  (RMSE ~25-28 m across all cases); it is *identity*, not *position*, that the
  crossing geometry breaks. This is the concrete, reproducible #11 evidence the
  ticket asked for.

- **PMBM (coverage_land) trades identity stability for cardinality/position
  looseness — neither tracker is a clean winner.** PMBM's Bernoulli-track
  identity is structurally stable: id-switches stay 0-8.3 across all 22 (max 8.3
  on imazu_22 vs MHT's 72). But it pays two ways:
  - a **persistent over-count**: card_err ≈ +0.77 even on clean single-target
    cases (it holds ~0.77 of an extra track), inflating OSPA to 130-260 m vs
    MHT's 37-195 m — consistent with the known PMBM over-birth behaviour on
    diffuse radar clutter (this arm uses λ_C = 2e-8 flat-Poisson; ~4 clutter
    plots/scan);
  - on the **densest 3-target cases** (14/15/17/19/20/22) it flips the other
    way — card_err ~0 or slightly negative (−0.11 on imazu_22) with lifetime
    dropping to 0.67-0.81 and track_breaks 20-32: it *under*-holds identity
    there, losing/re-acquiring tracks (breaks) rather than switching them.

  So: **MHT = accurate position, catastrophic identity churn on dense crossings;
  PMBM = stable identity, looser cardinality/position and a structural
  over-count.** A crisp, honest A/B for the arbiter — and the imazu family is now
  the controlled instrument for any future #11 (MHT association) or PMBM
  birth-model work.

- **Determinism** (a deliverable): fixtures are a pure function of `(scenario,
  seed)` — byte-identical on regeneration (spot-checked on imazu_01/12/22) and
  the in-memory `--verify` passes all 22; the bench replay is bit-stable (two
  MHT runs → identical non-timing metrics).
