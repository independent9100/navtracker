# 2026-07-08 — Imazu 22 encounter battery (identity through close crossings)

The 22 canonical **Imazu problem** encounter situations (Imazu, H., 1987, PhD
thesis, Univ. of Tokyo) transcribed as fixed-geometry sim scenarios
`imazu_01`..`imazu_22`: own-ship plus 1-3 target ships in every combination of
head-on / crossing / overtaking. This is a citable regression suite for exactly
what crossing geometry stresses in a tracker — **identity stability through
close passes**. Cases 1-4 are single-target (the four pure COLREG encounters),
5-11 two-target (7 cases), 12-22 three-target (11 cases): 4 + 7 + 11 = 22.

**Tracker-suite framing, not COLAV.** Own-ship steams a fixed course and never
manoeuvres; every target holds a constant course/speed. The encounters are
allowed to pass close or straight through collision geometry — that closeness at
CPA is the identity stress the suite exists to measure. Radar + AIS arm on every
vessel (no camera). Truth is the generator's independent ground truth (consumed
by no arm), so the accuracy numbers are honest by construction — the same
property `sim_multisensor` gives, applied to the crossing-geometry problem.

## Geometry source and conventions

Primary source: the **CORALL** repository `src/utils/imazu_cases.py`
(Klins101/CORALL, MIT), the runnable Sarhadi/Naeem-group encoding, verified by
direct download 2026-07-08. Its per-target positions/headings are transcribed
verbatim into `tests/fixtures/sim_multisensor/generator/imazu.py` (arithmetic
kept as-authored so it diffs against the source); the non-canonical `Case 23` is
dropped. CORALL frame: own-ship at (0,0) heading 0°=East; we keep that frame
(own-ship marine course 090) so CORALL `(x,y)` maps 1:1 onto ENU `(East,North)`.

**Speeds** — CORALL's hardcoded scalars (own 43.3, target 18.52) are
non-physical as m/s or kn, but their **ratio** is load-bearing: CORALL's
crossing geometry is tuned so a target collides with own-ship iff
`v_target/v_own = 18.52/43.3 = 0.4277` (check: Case 2 target at (5,-2.14) N-bound
reaches own's track when `2.14/v_t = 5/v_own`). We preserve that ratio and pick a
physical own-ship speed (**20 kn**, targets **8.6 kn**); absolute speed is a free
frame choice that leaves the encounters unchanged. Ranges are scaled ×0.5
(CORALL 3-7 NM → a realistic close-quarters 1.5-3.5 NM) so each case reaches CPA
in ~6-9 min; scaling is CPA-preserving up to the same factor and leaves all
bearings/courses/crossing angles identical.

**Sources disagree on scaling, not topology.** At least three independent numeric
encodings exist (CORALL; Waltz & Okhrin arXiv:2211.01004 Table D.1, own-North,
6.009 NM circle, has a Case-8==Case-5 typo; Xie JMSE 12(3):372, 21 rows only;
Lyu JMSE 12(8):1289, the only per-target knots table). They agree on the
topology (which encounters, the 4/7/11 grouping) and differ only on absolute
speed/frame/range — parameter-level, not geometry. Per the ticket we picked one
(CORALL, because it is verbatim-verifiable runnable code) and record the
divergence. Per-case COLREG-role tags below are **derived from geometry** (no
source publishes an explicit role table).

(local-only/git-ignored; `python -m generator.generate --family imazu`, see that
dir's README). Battery is 22 scenarios, seed 0. Metrics: GOSPA `c=20, p=α=2` (m);

## Reading the numbers (findings — no tuning)

Per the ticket, **no config was tuned to make cases pretty**; a case where
identity churns is a finding, reported with the geometry named.

- **Single-target cases are clean for both trackers.** imazu_01-04 (head-on,
  crossing-give-way, overtaking, crossing-stand-on — the four pure COLREG
  encounters, and note the tags come out canonically correct, an
  end-to-end sanity check of the geometry conversion): 0 id-switches, GOSPA
  15-20, lifetime ≥0.97. One target → no association ambiguity → no churn.

- **MHT identity churns hard on dense multi-target crossings — direct evidence
  for backlog #11 (bearing-driven identity churn).** id-switches climb with
  target count and crossing density, from 0 (single-target) to double digits
  (2-target) to the worst cases:
  - **imazu_17** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **72.0** mean switches/truth
  - **imazu_20** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **68.0**
  - **imazu_21** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **43.0**
  - **imazu_19** (overtaking ×2 + crossing-give-way): **42.3**
  - **imazu_14** (crossing-give-way ×2 + overtaking): **32.0**

  The pattern is unambiguous: the churn concentrates in the 3-target cases that
  put an **overtaken co-course target and one or more crossers converging on the
  same CPA region** (17/19/20/21 are the same overtaking-heavy family). When two
  or three targets pass within metres of each other and of own-ship — and radar
  cross-range error (~20-30 m at 1-2 NM) exceeds the true separation — MHT's
  per-hypothesis identity assignment thrashes. Position accuracy holds
  (RMSE ~25-28 m across all cases); it is *identity*, not *position*, that the
  crossing geometry breaks. This is the concrete, reproducible #11 evidence the
  ticket asked for.

- **PMBM (coverage_land) trades identity stability for cardinality/position
  looseness — neither tracker is a clean winner.** PMBM's Bernoulli-track
  identity is structurally stable: id-switches stay 0-8.3 across all 22 (max 8.3
  on imazu_22 vs MHT's 72). But it pays two ways:
  - a **persistent over-count**: card_err ≈ +0.77 even on clean single-target
    cases (it holds ~0.77 of an extra track), inflating OSPA to 130-260 m vs
    MHT's 37-195 m — consistent with the known PMBM over-birth behaviour on
    diffuse radar clutter (this arm uses λ_C = 2e-8 flat-Poisson; ~4 clutter
    plots/scan);
  - on the **densest 3-target cases** (14/15/17/19/20/22) it flips the other
    way — card_err ~0 or slightly negative (−0.11 on imazu_22) with lifetime
    dropping to 0.67-0.81 and track_breaks 20-32: it *under*-holds identity
    there, losing/re-acquiring tracks (breaks) rather than switching them.

  So: **MHT = accurate position, catastrophic identity churn on dense crossings;
  PMBM = stable identity, looser cardinality/position and a structural
  over-count.** A crisp, honest A/B for the arbiter — and the imazu family is now
  the controlled instrument for any future #11 (MHT association) or PMBM
  birth-model work.

- **Determinism** (a deliverable): fixtures are a pure function of `(scenario,
  seed)` — byte-identical on regeneration (spot-checked on imazu_01/12/22) and
  the in-memory `--verify` passes all 22; the bench replay is bit-stable (two
  MHT runs → identical non-timing metrics).

Fixture checksums are in `docs/algorithms/evaluation-log.md` (2026-07-08). The
committed tripwire (`tests/benchmark/test_imazu22_scenario_run.cpp`) asserts a
coarse per-case id-switch band (no exact pins — the knife-edge lesson holds), not
these values.

# 2026-07-08 (addendum) — backlog #11 churn diagnosis: which mechanism, PMBM mirror, and do the #11 knobs help here

This section diagnoses **why** the identity numbers above look the way they do.
It is a **diagnosis pass — no config or algorithm was changed.** The only new
code is three Python analysis scripts under `tools/`
(`imazu_switch_forensics.py`, `imazu_cardinality_timing.py`,
`imazu_trackloss.py`) that consume the bench's existing `--export-states-dir`
output (`scan,time_s,kind,id,east_m,north_m`); nothing in `core/`, `ports/`,
`adapters/`, `app/` was touched. Ticket:
`docs/superpowers/plans/2026-07-08-backlog11-imazu-churn-ticket.md`.

## Method and its faithfulness anchor

The 2026-06-12 sc5 "switch forensics" tooling was a throwaway (never committed;
only its numbers survive in the eval log). It was rebuilt as
`tools/imazu_switch_forensics.py`, which re-derives the **same** per-scan
truth→track assignment the bench uses to count identity metrics
(`core/benchmark/Metrics.cpp` `assignPerStep`: per-scan rectangular Hungarian on
ENU Euclidean distance, strict gate `d < assoc_gate_m` = 100 m; then
`computeContinuity`). The Hungarian is a line-for-line port of
`core/association/Hungarian.cpp` (Jonker–Volgenant, same padding / BIG_M /
tie-breaking).

**Faithfulness is proven, not assumed:** the script's per-truth `id_switches`
and `track_breaks` equal the bench's own `id_switches:truth_<id>` /
`track_breaks:truth_<id>` rows for **all 22 cases** (verified across MHT, PMBM,
and recapture runs — every per-truth value matches exactly, including
half-integer means such as imazu_09's 28.5). The event classification below is
therefore an exact **decomposition of the reported metric**, not a re-estimate.

Each id-switch (a *contiguous* assigned→assigned change of a truth's assigned
track id — the exact thing `computeContinuity` counts) is bucketed:
- **swap** — two truths mutually exchange two tracks at a close pass
  (g: X→Y at scan k while h: Y→X at scan ≈k);
- **conveyor / duplicate-track churn** — the truth hands off from an old track
  to one that was *born* within a few scans of the switch and/or the old track
  *dies* shortly after (`conveyor`), plus near-tie flips between co-existing
  duplicates (`flicker`) and hops among a truth's own established duplicate
  tracks (`other`). These three are the **same** duplicate-track pathology (the
  conveyor↔other boundary just moves with the birth/death window); they are
  reported together as "dup-churn".

Breaks (assigned→gap while the truth is present) are censused separately as
**break+re-confirm** (recover-same-id vs re-confirm-a-new-id). Note "events"
below is the **raw** event total = bench mean-per-truth × n_truth
(imazu_17: 216 = 72.0 × 3).

**Finding: the churn is the duplicate-track conveyor, dominant across the
family** — aggregated over all 18 multi-target cases, **duplicate-track churn is
88.9%** of id-switch events and genuine pair-swaps **11.1%**. Per case,
dup-churn is **≥76% on 17 of 18** cases; the sole exception is **imazu_08 at 65%
dup / 35% swap** (still dup-dominant). The swap fraction ranges 0–35% (0% on the
wide-CPA cases 05/10/13; 24% on imazu_19; 35% on imazu_08), so it is a genuine
but never-dominant channel. The split is threshold-robust: `dup-churn` stays
≥76% (aggregate) even with the tightest birth/death window, and `swap` is stable
under the classifier's thresholds (the ±1-scan swap tolerance under-counts by
<1%, in the conservative direction). The smoking gun is the **track-id proliferation** — up to **81
distinct track ids for 3 truths** (imazu_20) — the exact conveyor signature the
2026-06-12 sc5 diagnosis described (an overconfident track drifts, the sparse
radar return misses the χ² gate, a duplicate is born alongside, the young tree
confirms and takes the stream, the old tree starves → handoff every few scans).

**This is the same mechanism as sc5, but a different trigger.** sc5's conveyor
was rooted in camera-bearing filter over-confidence (fixed to a degree by the
item-12 honest-R work, 2026-06-16). Imazu carries **no camera** (radar+AIS
only), yet the conveyor still dominates on the *current* canonical — so here it
is driven by **radar cross-range ambiguity between targets that pass within the
gate**, not by bearing over-confidence. The ticket's hypothesis that dense
crossings would show *genuine* swaps "in a way sc5 didn't" is **confirmed but
secondary**: real swaps do appear (they didn't dominate sc5 either), but they
never exceed ~a third and the conveyor is always the main story.

**Geometry named (the three deep-dived cases).** Churn concentrates on whichever
target *pair* passes inside radar cross-range ambiguity (well within the 100 m
gate); an isolated target barely churns and never swaps:

- **imazu_17** (T1 overtaking, T2 overtaking, T3 crossing-give-way): T1–T2 pass
  **28.2 m** at scan 421 and, being co-course overtakers, stay mutually close
  for a long window; T3 stays 160–190 m off. **All 28 swaps are T1↔T2** (14 each;
  T3 = 0); dup-churn T1 = 88, T2 = 69, T3 = 31. Swaps are spread across the run
  (not one CPA spike) because the two overtakers are co-course.
- **imazu_20** (same T1/T2 overtaking + T3 crossing-give-way): T1–T2 **28.2 m** @
  421; T3 stays 231–295 m off. **All 22 swaps are T1↔T2** (11 each; T3 = 0);
  dup-churn T1 = 87, T2 = 78, T3 = 17.
- **imazu_14** (T1 crossing-give-way, T2 overtaking, T3 crossing-give-way): the
  two crossers T1–T3 pass **10.9 m** @ 475; T2 stays 51–142 m off. Very close
  pass → almost pure conveyor (swaps only 8), churn on the T1/T3 pair
  (dup-churn 42 / 21) more than on T2 (25).

Position holds throughout every case (RMSE 25–28 m); it is *identity*, not
*position*, that the geometry breaks. **break+re-confirm** is a real secondary
channel: on the churny 3-target cases roughly half of all breaks re-confirm to a
*new* track id (imazu_17: 48 of 96; imazu_14: 42 of 73).

## Q2 — PMBM's over-count is NOT the mirror of the swaps; it is clutter birth noise

`tools/imazu_cardinality_timing.py` decomposes PMBM cardinality per scan into
`extra` (confirmed tracks matched to no truth = over-count) and `missed` (present
truths matched to no track = under-count); `card_err = extra − missed` reproduces
the bench `card_err_mean`.

| case (PMBM) | closest truth-truth pass | mean tracks | card_err | = extra − missed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| imazu_01 (1 target, **no crossing possible**) | – | 1.766 | **+0.766** | 0.786 − 0.021 |
| imazu_08 (2 targets, pass **85 m**) | 85.2 m @ 435 | 2.850 | +0.850 | 0.903 − 0.053 |
| imazu_17 (3 targets, pass **28 m**) | 28.2 m @ 421 | 3.151 | +0.151 | 0.718 − 0.567 |
| imazu_22 (3 targets, pass **8 m**) | 7.9 m @ 314 | 2.892 | −0.108 | 0.890 − 0.999 |

**Finding: the ≈ +0.77 over-count is a crossing-independent baseline — it is
present, unchanged, on a single isolated target with no crossing at all**
(imazu_01: extra 0.786, misses ≈ 0). PMBM spawns a rotating cast of short-lived
duplicate Bernoullis — **39 distinct track ids for the one truth in imazu_01** —
while keeping a stable primary track (0 id-switches). This is the known PMBM
over-birth on diffuse radar clutter (this arm runs λ_C = 2e-8 flat Poisson,
≈ 4 clutter plots/scan); the extra tracks appear in bursts scattered across the
whole run, with **no relation to any crossing**. So the answer to the ticket's
Q2 is explicit: **the +0.77 is birth-model noise, not the cardinality-mirror of
MHT's identity ambiguity.**

The over-count is only half the PMBM story, and the smaller half. The baseline
over-count (extra ≈ 0.7–0.9) is essentially constant across every row above; what
changes with crossing closeness is the **miss** component — and that miss is
PMBM's real crossing failure mode, promoted to its own section next because it is
the operationally important finding.

## Q2b — PMBM's close-pass failure mode is *losing the track*, at the worst possible moment

The headline framing "PMBM holds identity, MHT churns" is true but hides the
sharper fact: **at a close pass PMBM's response is to drop the ambiguous target
for tens of seconds — right at the CPA, the moment a track matters most — and
frequently re-acquire it under a new id.** `tools/imazu_trackloss.py` finds every
*loss window* (a maximal run where a present truth is matched to no confirmed
track — i.e. exactly what the `track_breaks` metric counts), its duration, its
before→after track id, and whether it overlaps the CPA window (±30 s of the
target↔own-ship CPA and of the target↔target CPA). Own-ship position is taken
from the fixture `ownship.csv` (ENU about the datum).

| case (PMBM) | closest t↔t pass | own-ship CPA (per truth) | loss windows (=breaks) | total time lost (s) | longest loss | re-confirm new-id | substantial (≥10 s) / overlap own-CPA | headline lifetime |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| imazu_01 (1 target) | – | – | 5 | 10 | 3 s | 0 | 0 / 0 | 0.979 |
| imazu_08 (85 m pass) | 85 m @ 435 | 1–14 m @ 442–451 | 14 | 30 | 4 s | 0 | 0 / 0 | 0.974 |
| **imazu_14** (11 m) | 10.9 m @ 475 | 5–14 m @ 441–468 | 74 | 460 | 62 s | 9 | 12 / 4 | 0.782 |

Answering the four questions directly:

- **Which cases.** The six densest 3-target cases (14, 15, 17, 19, 20, 22) — the
  *same* six whose headline lifetime drops to 0.67–0.81 and whose card_err falls
  to ≈ 0/negative. Track loss is what *is* that lifetime drop. Cases with a wide
  or fleeting pass show none of it: the single-target and 85 m-pass controls have
  only 1–4 s flicker gaps, zero substantial losses, zero CPA overlap.
- **How long.** From 1–3 s flicker up to sustained losses of **62–158 s**. Each
  dense case has 6–16 *substantial* (≥10 s) losses and 398–709 s of total
  truth-unassigned time (summed over its three truths across the 720 s run).
- **Re-confirm under a new id?** Yes, routinely — 6–27 losses per dense case bring
  the target back under a *different* track id (imazu_22: 27; imazu_15: 18;
  imazu_20: 13), so the loss is often *also* an identity break. A handful are
  **permanent** (the truth is never re-acquired to run end: imazu_15 ×2,
  imazu_19 ×2, and one each on 14/20/22).
- **Overlap the CPA window?** Yes on all six dense cases. The substantial losses
  sit on the target↔target CPA (the ambiguity trigger) and, for 2–7 of them per
  case, on the **target↔own-ship CPA** — the collision-relevant moment. The two
  worst: imazu_15 loses a target for **158 s** and imazu_22 for **96 s**, both
  windows overlapping the own-ship CPA, both re-acquiring under a new id.

**Caveat (honest bound).** Closeness is necessary but not sufficient: imazu_12
has a 0.6 m target↔target pass yet keeps identity (lifetime 0.96, only 1
substantial loss, not overlapping own-CPA). The driver is *sustained* ambiguous
proximity (co-course pairs, as in the Q1 churn), not a single fleeting crossing —
consistent with the MHT picture. So the finding is "the dense sustained-proximity
cases", not "every close pass".

**So MHT and PMBM express the same crossing ambiguity in opposite currencies, and
both hurt at the CPA:** MHT keeps a track on the target but thrashes its identity
(churn, position held); PMBM keeps its primary id stable *away* from crossings but
at the close pass **drops the target entirely** (and often re-acquires a new id).
For an operator the two failure modes are not equivalent — see the recommendation.

long position-sensor gaps, which Imazu does not have.

## Recommendation for the arbiter — the trade, not a winner

This diagnosis deliberately does **not** declare "do nothing, PMBM is fine",
because Q2b makes that conclusion dishonest. The two trackers fail the *same*
close-crossing geometry in two different currencies, and the choice between them
is a genuine operational trade the arbiter/user must make, not a metric the data
settles:

- **MHT failure mode: identity churn, position preserved.** At a close pass MHT
  keeps a track continuously on the target (lifetime high, RMSE 25–28 m) but
  reshuffles which id it wears — up to 72 switches/truth, dominated by the
  duplicate-track conveyor. *Operational cost:* the target stays on screen and in
  the collision picture throughout, but its label/history is unreliable — bad for
  anything keyed on stable identity (per-track CPA history, operator "that's the
  same vessel" continuity, AIS-name binding).
- **PMBM failure mode: track loss, identity of the survivor preserved.** PMBM
  keeps its primary id stable away from crossings, but at the close pass it
  *drops* the target — tens of seconds, up to 158 s, overlapping the own-ship
  CPA, often re-acquiring under a new id (Q2b). *Operational cost:* the target
  can vanish from the picture precisely at the CPA — the worst moment — which is a
  safety-relevant gap, not merely a bookkeeping one. Its steady-state also carries
  a crossing-independent ≈ +0.77 clutter over-count.

**How to weigh it:** if the consuming system most needs *continuous presence
through the encounter* (collision avoidance, "never lose a contact near CPA"),
MHT's churn is the lesser evil and PMBM's close-pass dropout is the disqualifier.
If it most needs *stable identity for the tracks it does hold* and can tolerate a
brief dropout, PMBM wins. Neither is free on this geometry. The arbiter and user
own this call; the report's job is to frame it, which it now does.

Given that, the ranked engineering actions:

1. **Close the #11 "MHT identity churn" line as *diagnosed*** (mechanism:
   radar-cross-range duplicate-track conveyor, 88.9% of events; same shape as sc5,
   different trigger; genuine swaps 11.1%, confined to sustained-proximity pairs;
   position never at risk). This does not by itself pick a tracker.
2. **Elevate PMBM close-pass track-loss as the more decision-relevant finding**
   (Q2b). Arbiter's call whether it becomes a new backlog item or attaches to an
   existing PMBM-birth/continuity line; it is a PMBM birth/continuity question,
   *not* MHT association, and it is the thing that would actually change a
   deployment choice.
3. **The crossing-independent +0.77 over-count** is the separate clutter/birth
   channel already explored and parked (measured-negative 2026-07-07; the
   λ_C-cancels-in-birth invariant) — noted, not re-opened here.
4. **None of the #11 knobs is promotable.** Recapture is net-negative on Imazu;
   share-bearings is inapplicable (no bearings); per-sensor gate widening was not
   re-measured (forbidden config edit) and prior evidence says it trades lifetime.
   Leave all three OFF.
5. **If MHT identity is ever the chosen path**, the lever is **duplicate-birth
   suppression near an existing confirmed track** (birth gating), *not* gate
   widening — a new MHT-association ticket, out of scope here.
