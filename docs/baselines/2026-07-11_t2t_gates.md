## Checkpoint-2 number — scenario 2, CI vs naive NEES

The double-counting hazard: two trackers that both fused the **same AIS stream**
have correlated errors. Naive (independence-assuming) fusion counts that shared
AIS twice → the fused covariance is too small (overconfident). CI does not.

Position NEES (2-DoF; consistent ≈ 2), `sim_ms_headon` s0:

| Arm split | Rule | NEES n | NEES mean | NEES median | cov95 | naive/CI mean |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Maximal** (A=AIS, B=AIS — both fuse the same AIS) | CI | 894 | 0.482 | 0.206 | 1.00 | — |
| | naive | 894 | 0.824 | 0.255 | 0.984 | **1.71×** |
| Partial (A=radar+AIS, B=AIS — radar dilutes A) | CI | — | — | 1.283 | — | — |
| | naive | — | — | 1.221 | — | ~1.0 (median) |

**Reading it honestly:**

- **Maximal sharing** is the clean gate: two trackers on the same AIS. Naive
  fusion is **1.71× more overconfident** than CI (mean NEES) — close to the
  theoretical 2× for fusing a duplicated estimate. The below-2 value is because
  birth/pairing transients leave some scans with a single contributor (ratio 1
  there), diluting the aggregate; on the scans where both arms genuinely
  co-fuse the ratio is ~2. CI stays consistent throughout.
- **Absolute NEES < 1** (CI median 0.21, cov95 1.00) means the per-tracker AIS
  covariances are *conservative* (under-confident) on this sim. So the
  double-counting shows as a **confidence-inflation ratio**, not a χ²
  band-violation: halving an already-loose covariance still lands inside the
  band. If the inputs were calibrated (NEES ≈ 2), the same 1.71× would push
  naive to NEES ≈ 3.4 — a clear band violation. The ratio is the invariant
  evidence; the band-violation is a consequence only under calibrated inputs.
  This is why the gate asserts the ratio (naive mean ≥ 1.4× CI), not a band.
- **Partial sharing** (the ticket's literal A=radar+AIS / B=AIS): radar
  dominates A, so the shared-AIS correlation is small and naive ≈ CI. This is a
  characterization row, not a gate — it shows the operational magnitude on a
  radar-dominated tracker, where double-counting is mild.

## §10 Rider A — covariance-axis convention (verified, NEES path is ENU-only)

The pre-release review flagged (HIGH, CONFIRMED) that `TrackOutput`'s position
covariance axis order is not trustworthy from the header. Verified **empirically**
(`toTrackOutput` probe, target at datum origin so the datum rotation is identity):

- **Emitted order is ENU `(east, north)`** — slot (0,0)=east var, (1,1)=north
  var. The header's claim of "local NED (north-east)" is **wrong**: `toGeodeticWithCov`
  applies only a small convergence-angle *rotation* `R·cov·Rᵀ`, never an axis
  relabel. (This is the confirmed upstream mismatch; not ours to fix.)
- **The Checkpoint-2 NEES number never touches that field.** The pipeline is
  ENU end-to-end: `NavtrackerSource`/`feedStep` copy the raw ENU track covariance
  (`.topLeftCorner<2,2>()`), CI fuses in ENU, scoring reads `fusedTracksEnu()`,
  and `computeNees` forms `eᵀP⁻¹e` on ENU error vs ENU truth. `toTrackOutput`/
  `toGeodeticWithCov` are not on the path. So a silent axis swap upstream cannot
  corrupt these numbers.
- **Pinned by test** `TrackOutputCovarianceAxis.EmittedOrderingIsEnuEastNorthNotHeaderNed`
  (+ `NavtrackerSource.CopiesRawEnuCovarianceBypassingTrackOutput`) in
  `tests/t2t/test_navtracker_source.cpp`. If the upstream fix wave ever makes the
  code emit true NED, the first test flips RED — the flip becomes loud, not silent.

## §10 Rider B — pedigree assertions are handcrafted-only (compliant)

`contributing_sources` can carry spurious entries upstream (PmbmTracker:1666).
No M3 test asserts pedigree *content* from live tracker output: `test_navtracker_source`
uses handcrafted `Track`s; scenario `independence_class` is derived from the
ArmSpec-*declared* pedigree (`usedStreams`/`allUnknown`), and `feedStep` sets no
pedigree at all. Live tracker `contributing_sources` never becomes an assertion.
(M4's full-stack test must likewise assert pedigree *plumbing* only, not content.)

## Point-2 calibration — the double-count as a real χ² band violation

The per-arm NEES ≪ 1 above is not a fusion property; it is an **input-calibration**
artefact. The shared AIS loader stamps every AIS fix with a 30 m pessimistic σ
(right for real, stale AIS), but this sim injects **no additive AIS position
noise** — only ~0.19 m lat/lon quantization. So each AIS arm is ~150× under-
confident. Calibrating the AIS σ **test-locally** (`withAisSigma` in the harness;
no generator, fixture, or shared-loader change) so each arm's own NEES ≈ 2 turns
the gate from a ratio into a band violation. Sweep, `sim_ms_headon` s0, maximal
sharing (both arms fuse only the shared AIS):

| AIS σ (m) | per-arm NEES mean | median | cov95 | CI mean | CI band | CI cov95 | naive mean | naive cov95 | naive/CI |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **30** (loader default — *operational*, real AIS) | 0.42 | 0.20 | 1.00 | 0.48 | [1.87, 2.13] | 1.00 | 0.82 | 0.984 | 1.71× |
| **16** (*coverage-calibrated*: CI cov95 ≈ 0.95) | 1.32 | 0.46 | 0.948 | 1.33 | [1.87, 2.13] | 0.948 | 2.66 | 0.824 | 2.00× |
| **12** (*NEES ≈ 2 calibrated* — the gate) | 2.06 | 0.50 | 0.902 | **2.08** | **[1.87, 2.14]** | 0.901 | **3.16** | 0.826 | 1.52× |
| 10 | 2.90 | 0.58 | 0.846 | 2.99 | — | 0.839 | 4.60 | 0.755 | 1.54× |
| 8 | 4.35 | 0.82 | 0.784 | 4.45 | — | 0.777 | 6.86 | 0.693 | 1.54× |

**Reading it:**

- At **σ = 12 m** the AIS arm's own mean NEES = 2.06 ≈ 2 (the arbiter's target).
  There the double-count is a genuine **band violation**: CI mean **2.08 sits
  inside** the χ² band [1.87, 2.14]; naive mean **3.16 breaches** band_hi. CI
  consistent, naive overconfident — exactly the shape the preliminary ratio-only
  gate could not show. Pinned by `T2tScenarioRun.PerArmNeesCalibrationAndBandViolationGate`.
- **Honest caveat (fragility):** the fused-NEES distribution is heavy-tailed
  (median ≈ 0.5 ≪ mean ≈ 2 at σ=12) — a few birth/coast/association transients
  with a now-tight covariance dominate the mean. So "CI mean in band" is
  **tail-driven and knife-edge** in σ. The gate therefore hard-asserts only the
  *robust, calibration-invariant* facts: (a) naive ≥ 1.4× CI at the mean, (b)
  naive breaches band_hi, (c) CI covers truth strictly better than naive
  (coverage is outlier-insensitive).
- **Robust invariants** (hold at *every* σ): naive/CI mean ratio ≈ 1.5–2.0× (the
  ~2× double-count factor), and naive cov95 < CI cov95 monotonically. The σ=16
  *coverage*-calibrated row is the cleanest robust view: CI cov95 0.95 (perfect),
  naive 0.82 (under-covers), ratio exactly 2.00.
- **Decision for the arbiter:** which becomes the *formal* gate — the σ=12
  mean-band violation (matches the "NEES ≈ 2" recipe but rides a fragile mean),
  or the σ=16 coverage crossover (robust, but a coverage-analog not a mean-band)?
  The σ=30 operational row stays regardless (it is the real-AIS deployment case).
  No generator change was needed, so the ticket's stop-and-report trigger was
  **not** hit.
