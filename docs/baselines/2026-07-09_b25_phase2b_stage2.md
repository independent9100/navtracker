## What shipped

A per-instance, ctor-threaded, **default-OFF** kinematic guard in `PmbmTracker`:
at a detection update whose position innovation `‖z − Hx̂⁻‖ > innov_gate_max_m`,
**accept the position but treat the velocity/turn-rate** (across the moment-
matched mean/cov AND every IMM mode). Config: `innov_gate_max_m` (≤0 = off),
`innov_gate_action ∈ {kVelocityDeweight (default), kVelocityReset}`,
`innov_gate_velocity_var_floor` (1e4 (m/s)²). Existence/mass/birth/id untouched.

**Shipped choice (rider 1 — pick by measurement): `deweight`, `D_max = 400 m`.**
Wired as the named bench config `imm_cv_ct_pmbm_coverage_land_ivgate` (the library
default keeps the guard OFF). `coverage_land`'s PMBM knobs were factored into
`makeCoverageLandPmbmConfig()` so the gated variant shares them verbatim; the
extraction is a proven no-op (`coverage_land` byte-identical to the pre-2b binary).

## Rider 1 — A/B the velocity treatment (reset vs deweight × D200/D400)

Six dying cases (imazu_15 truths 151/152/153, imazu_22 truths 221/222/223),
seed 0, agreed gates = loss-seconds-overlapping-CPA + re-acquire-id count:

| variant | dying loss (s) | **CPA-overlap loss (s)** | re-acquire ids | id-switches all / dense | gospa_mean Σ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| OFF | 1366 | **163** | 45 | 34 / 30 | 589.3 |
| reset D200 | 630 | 51 | 9 | 13 / 12 | 575.6 |
| reset D400 | 550 | 299 | 14 | 17 / 13 | 582.8 |
| deweight D200 | 939 | 179 | 17 | 9 / 8 | 557.5 |
| **deweight D400** | **544** | **6** | 10 | 15 / 11 | 574.9 |

**Winner: deweight @ D_max 400 m.** It collapses the CPA-overlap loss (the
safety-critical metric — target absent *at* the CPA) from 163 s to **6 s**
(per-case ≤ 3 s on all six — the Q2b 158 s / 96 s blackouts essentially
eliminated), with the lowest total dying loss and re-acquire 45 → 10.

**Why deweight beats reset (the mechanism):** reset zeroes the velocity → the
track *stalls* while the real target keeps moving → it stays lost through the CPA
(reset D400 CPA-overlap 299 s, *worse* than OFF). Deweight keeps the velocity
mean but widens its covariance → the track keeps moving in roughly the target's
direction and the wide gain re-locks onto it. Deweight is also the rider's
less-opinionated default. (Ties never arose — deweight wins the safety gate
outright.)

## Rider 2 — id-switch watch-item (accept-position can snap onto the neighbour)

Measured id-switches ON vs OFF explicitly on the dense Imazu cases: **every
variant REDUCES id-switches** (OFF 34/30 all/dense → deweight D400 15/11). **No
swap regression** — the guard cuts the runaway-then-reacquire-under-new-id churn.
The neighbour-snap risk is real in principle (the parked **coalescence guard** is
its companion lever) but does **not** materialise as a net id-switch rise here;
recorded as evidence, scope not expanded.

## Rider 3 — bench 200 AND 400 + the phantom-lifetime bonus

Both bands benched (table above): D200 is marginal (deweight D200 CPA-overlap
179 s ≈ OFF); **D400 is the clean band** on both the safety gate and (Stage 1)
false-fire. The census's 82.5 % clutter-born phantom majority: the guard is
**presence-neutral**, not a phantom killer —

| imazu, OFF → deweight D400 | OFF | d400 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| clutter-born confirmed tracks | 944 | 819 | −125 |
| clutter median lifetime (s) | 81 | 120 | +39 |
| clutter total track-seconds (≈ count × mean) | ~88.0k | ~88.5k | ≈ 0 |
| target-born confirmed tracks | 44 | 65 | +21 |

Fewer clutter phantoms confirm, but survivors persist longer (the guard keeps a
kinematically-runaway phantom present instead of flinging it off — existence
untouched by design), so **total phantom presence is ≈ unchanged**. The bonus
does **not** decide the band; the CPA-overlap gate does. (The `speed > 50 m/s`
"runaway rows" count *rises* under deweight — expected: deweight tolerates a
transient velocity-state spike while the position is held correct; it is not a
fly-off. The position-based CPA-overlap metric is the honest one.)

## Placement recap (Stage 1, confirmed)

The runaway is a moderate-innovation (50–108 m) **CT-mode** velocity build-up
(speed 2 → 110+ m/s) followed by one **oversized accepted innovation** (imazu_15
id 6: 484 m at t = 355 s). The gate triggers cleanly at update-acceptance on the
oversized innovation; deweight recovers despite the earlier build-up because the
wide velocity prior + subsequent positions pull the speed back. The CV↔CT
mode-thrash signature (`imm_mode_weights`) is the lead for the escalation lever
(a CT-mode-keyed estimator clamp) if a workload is ever found where deweight does
not recover — not needed on any workload measured here.
